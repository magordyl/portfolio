The Weekly

TL;DR: An automated system for generating lean, high-protein meal plans based on cyclical ingredient logistics.

PROBLEM
Modern meal planning often collapses under the weight of decision fatigue and logistical waste. Most systems prioritize variety over sustainability, leading to orphaned ingredients and escalating costs. "The Weekly" was built to reverse this entropy by treating groceries as immutable state in a recurring cycle.
DESIGN

The interface follows a brutalist hierarchy, stripping away imagery to focus entirely on nutritional typography and quantity-driven logistics.

ARCHITECTURE

The backend operates on a unidirectional data flow. Ingredients are registered as primitives, which are then composed into 'MealStates'. This allows the system to calculate the delta between the desired nutrition and the current pantry status with mathematical certainty.

DECISIONS

How do we solve the 'Half-Onion' problem? We move the complexity from the user to the algorithm.

The following log captures the moment the core logic shifted toward ingredient-first consolidation.

DYLAN

The current recipe-first model is creating too much waste. If two recipes use 200g and 150g of chicken, but it's sold in 500g packs, the system ignores the leftover 150g. Can we flip the logic?

Plan: ingredient consolidation approach expand_more
1. Aggregate total SKU volumes from selected recipes.
2. Calculate modulo against standard retail packaging units.
3. Inject "filler snacks" or "side components" to absorb delta residues.
4. Return optimized shopping list.

OUTCOME

The resulting platform reduced grocery waste by 40% in internal testing and cut the time spent on meal planning from 45 minutes to under 30 seconds.

LESSONS

User experience isn't always about adding features; sometimes it's about removing variables. By constraining the user's choices to only those that were logistically sound, we paradoxically increased their satisfaction.
