Planner V1 · Design Explorer v4 · 2026-04-12

Forest, grounded. Six ways to celebrate.

v3's Spring Beech was closest but slightly too bright, and v3's celebration hues were still clashing. v4 holds the primary steady at a single forest green — sitting between v2's muted pine and v3's bright beech — and runs six celebration options against it. The first three are monochromatic: same hue, brighter and more saturated for the gamification moments. The next three swap only the celebration for a non-clashing alternative.

Colour theory, applied → A monochromatic pairing is the safest possible harmony: vary saturation + lightness within a single hue and the eye reads it as the same thing, excited. Analogous pairings (hues adjacent on the wheel — green→teal→cyan) are the second safest because they share undertones. Complementary pairings (green ↔ red/pink) are high-contrast but vibrate visually — that's what v3's pink-on-green was doing, and why it read as clash. v4 stays away from complementary entirely. Every celebration hue in this round is either the same hue as the primary (monos) or cool-analogous to it (the alternates).
Row 1 · Monochromatic — darker green + brighter green

Same hue family for both roles. Primary does the grounding, celebration does the lift. The safest kind of harmony — zero chance of clash because there's only one colour doing two jobs.

How to read this round

Before choosing, sanity-check the primary. All six frames share the same forest green at rest (check the FAB, active tab dot, and done-task checkmarks). If that green is wrong, say so and I'll shift it before exploring any celebration variation further. It's the 60% of the palette — celebration is the 10% — so getting the primary right matters most.

Row 1 — the three monos. Same hue, different temperature:

  • 01 Forest × Spring — central forest, the baseline. Neither cool nor warm.
  • 02 Cedar × Jade — pushed ~15° toward blue. More conifer, more Pacific Northwest.
  • 03 Oak × Meadow — pushed ~15° toward yellow. More deciduous, more spring-canopy.

Row 2 — the three alternates. All use 01's primary unchanged. Only the celebration hue differs, and all three stay on the cool side of the wheel so nothing vibrates against the green:

  • 04 Forest × Sky — cyan. Analogous (green → teal → cyan). The most natural pair of the three.
  • 05 Forest × Periwinkle — soft blue-violet. Most distinctive; nothing in the category uses this.
  • 06 Forest × Teal — the closest hop on the wheel. Most energetic of the alternates because the hue actually moves, but it moves toward green's nearest neighbour, not away.

Why no warm celebrations this round. Every warm celebration we've tried — amber (v2), blossom pink (v3), citron (v3) — has clashed against a cool primary. That's not accident, it's the colour-theory physics: warm-on-cool at similar saturations reads as vibration, and the eye reports "clash." v4 cuts the whole warm-on-cool axis out and explores only monos (same hue) and cool analogues (same temperature). If none of these land, the next useful redirect is probably to change the primary, not keep searching the celebration space.
